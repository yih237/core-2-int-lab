Lecture Response 1
Response to: CD Lecture Series: Elaine Lopez

The lecture explored the importance of celebrating cultural identities in design and the challenges designers face in a white-centric industry. Elaine Lopez emphasized broadening perspectives, challenging norms, and telling new stories to create a more inclusive and equitable design field. Her presentation included examples of Lopez's work, including projects that centered on the cultural identities of her clients and her own.

Hung Liu, a Chinese-American artist, was the subject of one of her most notable projects. With equal emphasis on Chinese and English text, Lopez's exhibition design was intentionally bilingual. This design choice encourages visitors to engage with both languages equally to honor Liu's identity as an American and Chinese artist. An additional project Lopez shared was a workshop featuring Philadelphia-based activists designed to provide guidance and support to students on college campuses.

Regardless of designers' challenges in creating culturally relevant work within a homogenous industry, Lopez stressed the importance of reconnecting with one's culture through design. In addition, it is challenging to balance personal priorities with corporate constraints, such as cultural identity and activism. Aside from self-publishing, she suggested designers find other passion projects to satisfy their creative urges. Ultimately, Lopez's lecture offered designers an inspiring call to action to use their skills to celebrate cultural identities and push for an inclusive, equitable design industry.
