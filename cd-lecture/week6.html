Lecture Response 3
Response to: CD Lecture Series: Pascal Glissmann

Pascal Glissmann's lecture was incredibly inspirational and thought-provoking for me. In addition to being creative, Glissmann emphasizes the importance of community and observation in design.

Glissmann presented several of his recent design projects that focus on observation. The Atlas of Everyday Objects documents everyday objects during the pandemic, the Global Experiment on Looking at Water, invites people to observe and document water in their local environments, and the Typographic Matchmaking Project, brings together designers from diverse cultures to design typefaces.

In particular, I was interested in his Atlas of Looking at Water, which encourages people to observe and document water in their environment. Along with other projects he presented, this project showed me how design can be used to engage people in social and environmental issues.

Glissmann's emphasis on community building resonated with me as well. Relationships between designers, collaborators, and stakeholders are essential for a successful project, he said. The focus on community and collaboration reminded me that design isn't just about aesthetics or problem-solving; it's about creating relationships.

In summary, Glissmann's presentation reminded me that design can contribute to social and environmental change. Furthermore, I became more aware of the importance of community and observation in my own design practice.
