Lecture Response 2
Response to: CD Lecture Series: Lynn Kiang

Lynn Kiang spoke about her design work, precisely her approach to projects and processes. Clients' needs and concerns are important to her, as well as trust and collaboration. She stressed the importance of understanding the context of the project, including the space it will occupy, the audience it will reach, and the story it will tell. To find the best solution, she emphasized asking questions, doing research, and experimenting.

In addition, Lynn talked about how she found work through word-of-mouth and building relationships with clients. The protest posters she created in response to the 2016 presidential election garnered a significant comeback and emphasized the power of design.

She also discussed her project with the Museum of the City of New York, a 100th-anniversary exhibition. The exhibition will feature interactive media pieces telling the New York City story. Lynn and her team use various media, including projections, sound, and books.

Aspiring designers can learn a lot from Lin Kiang's lecture. One key takeaway is the importance of trust in building solid relationships with clients and collaborators. Lin stressed the importance of word-of-mouth recommendations and building a solid reputation. Additionally, Lin's poster design for the Women's March demonstrated the power of design for activism. Adaptability is another key takeaway, such as navigating exhibit space constraints or responding to unexpected political conditions. Lin's focus on low-fi, rapid-response design approaches offers a reminder that effective design doesn't always require a large team or the latest technology but rather a thoughtful and strategic approach. Lin notes that "it doesn't take many people to do complex stuff. You just need the right people." Another quote that stood out was, "Design at its most fundamental is there to communicate." This highlights the importance of design in giving people a voice and telling stories that need to be heard.
