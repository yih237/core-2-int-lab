Reading Response 4
Response to: Black Gooey Universe
by American Artist

"Black Gooey Universe" by Rashid Johnson examines identity and materiality. Johnson uses shea butter and black soap to get people to think about how the things around us affect our bodies and our sense of who we are. The installation challenges our assumptions about art materials and makes us think about culture, race, and history.


The installation is physically impressive. The vast, room-sized structure of many different materials draws people in and makes them want to explore and interact. The installation compels the audience to touch, smell, and interact with the objects. This physical contact with the art reminds us that our identities are tied to our bodies and shows how our senses and physical experiences shape how we see the world. It makes us think about our bodies and experiences in a deeply personal and universal way. Johnson makes a space for people to think and reflect by asking them to use their senses and personal experiences to interact with his art. He also encourages us to learn more about the rich and complicated histories that have shaped our world. Through its use of tactile, sensory materials and its physical, immersive structure, the installation gives a unique and thought-provoking look at how race, culture, and materiality intersect.


"Black Gooey Universe" forces us to consider race, culture, and materiality. The installation's tactile, sensory materials and immersive structure encourage spectators to experience these complicated concepts. Johnson's work inspires us to study our world's rich and complex history by asking us to reflect on our bodies and experiences.
