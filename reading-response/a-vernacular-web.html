Reading Response 1
Response to:A Vernacular Web by Olia Lialina

This article discusses the order of technological advances during the digital revolution of typography. In 1985, Apple’s LaserWriter printer was developed, and to showcase the capabilities, Apple developed page layout applications in collaboration with software companies. This led to the start of digital typography design. The digital construction of letterforms began in the early 80s, and in 1986 Lucida was introduced to suit printing and screen display. Chicago and Geneva are typefaces developed to suit the need of the early mac’s system. Graphic designers started to digitalize their designs starting in 1985, designing typefaces that exploited the look of a pixel and creating a magazine to showcase these fonts. Antialiasing - blurring the edge of a letter into the background - was introduced in 1987, and later used in Photoshop. Adobe’s Photoshop and Illustrator, a photo retouching and drawing tool that supported the PostScript printer, was later introduced. Due to the introduction of the CD, graphic design now has to cover multimedia platforms, and there was a large need for digital fonts. Apple and Adobe quickly became the two heads of designing and digitalizing typefaces. After the prevalence of the CD-ROM market, the World Wide Web was introduced in the mid1990s, and more complex typographic choices were required for graphic designers. Designers saved their typefaces as graphic files using Photoshop due to the option to override the designer’s specifications in early HTML. Designers were allowed to conceptualize and realize letters in refreshing ways due to advancements in technology, and it revised the status of the written word in the late 20th century.
